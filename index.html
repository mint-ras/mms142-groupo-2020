Augmented Reality

World of Extended Reality

Augmented reality is when parts of the users’ physical world is enhanced or extended with the use of computer-generated input. These inputs can be sound, video, graphics, GPS overlays and much more. This digital content then responds in real time to changes in the user’s environment, such as movement. The term “augmented reality” was first used to describe such technology in 1990 by Thomas Caudell.

Augmented Reality (AR) – Digital elements appear over real-world views, sometimes with limited interaction between them (often via smartphones).
Virtual Reality (VR) – An immersive experiences that isolates users from the real world (typically using headset devices).
Mixed Reality (MR) – A combination of AR & VR elements so digital objects can interact with the real world.

There is a slight overlap regarding interactivity. But in AR, digital elements are merely super-imposed on real world views, not anchored directly to them.

Augmented Reality Applied


Retail

AR allows users to try on virtually anything in retail from clothes to shoes to glasses to seeing how furniture would look in your home. Some retailers employing AR include Converse, Vyking, Nike, Watchbox, Speqs Eyewear, Warby Parker, IKEA, just to name a few. 72% of customers purchased products they hadn’t planned on after using AR while shopping, according to the study “The Impact of AR on Retail.” 72% of customers purchased products they hadn’t planned on after using AR while shopping, according to the study “The Impact of AR on Retail.”


Construction & Training

In construction, AR allows architects, clients, crews, and developers to visualize a design in a space & existing conditions before any construction begins. AR & MR also provide valuable training tools for factory workers & technicians to complete tasks, troubleshoot problems, & digital references.


Tourism

AR can provide a potential traveler with an even more immersive experience from a destination before they travel.


Education

AR can help engage students with dynamic 3D models & benefit visual learners with digital renderings & life-like visualizations.


Healthcare

AR can provide surgeons with digital images & critical information within their field of view during a surgical procedure. Startups are building AR technology to support digital surgery, 3D medical imaging & specific surgeries.


Transportation and Navigation Systems

AR makes it possible for transportation-objects to project the outside camera view onto the interior seats giving the illusion of a transporting device.

Virtual Reality as Disruptive Innovation

What is Disruptive Innovation?

Disruptive innovation describes a product, service or technology that initially takes root in simple applications at the bottom of a market, and then “relentlessly moves upmarket, eventually displacing established competitors”, according the Christensen Institute.


Disruptive innovations are not breakthrough technologies that make good products better but instead, they are innovations that make products and services more accessible and affordable, making them available to a larger population.

Augmented Reality, Virtual Reality, and Mixed Reality as Disruptive Innovations

Augmented, virtual, and mixed reality products are at the forefront of immense and dramatic technological progress. Virtual reality has been described as the most disruptive technology of the next decade by IDTechEX reports because it has already had a major impact in a range of different industries. In its recent market research report, IDTechEx predicts this technology will grow to $8 billion by 2030.


The new capabilities that augmented and virtual reality will usher in the next wave of massive disruption. These capabilities include but are not limited to remote working, digital twins, paperless, environments and hands-free working environments.

High levels of funding and investment were put into virtual, augmented, and mixed reality products during through 2010-2020 and is predicted to continue into the next decade. IDTechEx also forecasts that AR/VR and MR collectively combined will be over $30 billion by 2030. The impact of these technologies will impact a diverse range of industries, from retail, entertainment, education, medicine, and tourism. Virtual reality and augmented reality has been wide-reaching and will continue to be innovate and accelerate innovation in these industries.
